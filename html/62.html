62

GO, LABOUR ON

1. Go, labour on, spend, and be spent, Thy joy to do the Father's will: It is the way the Master went; Should not the servant tread it still?

2. Go, labour on! 'tis not for nought Thy earthly loss is heav'nly gain; Men heed thee, love thee, praise thee not; The Master praises, what are men?

3. Men die in darkness at thy side, Without a hope to cheer the tomb; Take up the torch and wave it wide, The torch that lights time's thickest gloom.

4. Toil on, and in thy toil rejoice, For toil comes rest, for exile home; Soon shalt thou hear the Bridegroom's voice, The midnight peal, "Behold, I come!"

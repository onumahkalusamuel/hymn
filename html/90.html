O LOVE THAT WILT NOT LET ME GO

1. O Love that wilt not let me go, I rest my weary soul in thee; I give thee back the life I owe, That in Thine ocean depths its flow May richer, fuller be.

2. O light that follow'st all my way, I yield my flick'ring torch to thee; My heart restores its borrow'd ray, That in thy sunshine's blaze its day May brighter, fairer be.

3. O Joy that seekest me through pain, I cannot close my heart to Thee; I trace the rainbow through the rain, And feel the promise is not vain, That morn shall tearless be.

4. O Cross that liftest up my head, I dare not ask to fly from Yhee; I lay in dust life's glory dead, And from the ground there blossoms red Life that shall endless be.
